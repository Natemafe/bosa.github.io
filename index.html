Do Well And Doubt Not

Why is an association such as
BOSA important to a school?

An Old Students Association is supposed to work on behalf of former students, donors and friends, connecting them with the school, whilst encouraging a lifelong association between all the stakeholders.

BOSA supports our alma mater so that the next generation of students gets the best foundation in their early development. Every Blakiston Junior School student should have an equal opportunity to become whatever individual they aspire to be.

We see our role becoming increasingly important, as we collaborate with the school administration, as it aims to bring the best Blakiston experience possible to the current and future generations of students.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Blakiston Old Students Association?

Its an Association that is made up of former students of Blakiston Junior School, donors and friends worldwide.

Is membership only restricted to former students?

Membership is open to former students, or anyone with some significant connection with the school whether as a past or current parent, benefactor, donor or friend.

Who manages the donations made to the Association?

The authority to administer BOSA Trust affairs shall be derived from the AGM. The AGM shall in turn mandate BOSA Trust to operate through an Administration of elected members at the Annual General Meeting.

Is the School aware of the Association?

The school knows about the Association and there is constant communication between the two. BOSA expects a lifelong collaboration between the Association and the school.

Are there any plans to also co-ordinate with the PTA or Parents?

The Association is open to the idea of working with anyone who has the best interests of the school at heart.
